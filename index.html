Carbon Emissions Around the World

An interactive visualisation by Justin Barugahare


Rising Hazardous Emissions







Global warming has become a more concerning issue over the years. Taking a look at CO2 Emissions from different countries, we notice a trend with developing and developed countries where many developing countries (e.g. such as Tanzania) tend to have low CO2 Emissions per person in comparison to developed countries (e.g. such as Australia and Canada).

Hazardous Carbon Emissions







This makes sense as individuals from developed countries would spend a lot more energy and use transport more often than those from developing countries.

The graph provides an overview of carbon emissions from different countries from 1990-2019. Click and drag over the bottom graph to change the time period of the top graph to get a more detailed view of trends.

Rankings of Carbon Emisssions per Capita







A study conducted last year stated that in order to safely fall within the goal set by 2015 governments to limiting global warming to 1.5 degrees celsius, we would need to have every person emit on average 2.3 tons of CO2 per year by 2030. As shown by the accompanying graph, many countries are unfortunately not reaching this goal currently.
